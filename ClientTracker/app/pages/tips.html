✅ What works
  • Lead with a specific compliment — mention an actual video, edit choice, or hook that stood out, not a generic "love your content."
  • Offer a free sample edit on one of their existing clips. Seeing your work on their own footage converts far better than a portfolio link.
  • Target accounts in the 1K–100K follower range — big enough to be posting regularly, small enough that editing help still feels like a real unlock.
  • Follow up 2–3 times before moving on. Most replies come after the second touch, not the first message.
  • Keep your first message under 3 sentences. Long pitches read as spammy and get skipped.
  • Match your outreach tone to their content — playful creators respond to playful DMs, serious/educational creators respond to a more professional tone.
  • Track everything. Knowing exactly who you've contacted and when is what makes consistent outreach possible.
⚠️ Mistakes to avoid
  • Don't send the same generic template to everyone — creators can tell, and it tanks your reply rate.
  • Don't lead with pricing. Show value first with a sample; talk numbers once they're interested.
  • Don't disappear after one no-reply. A polite follow-up is expected, not annoying.
  • Don't over-promise turnaround times you can't hit — a missed deadline on the first project ends the relationship fast.
  • Don't skip the discovery questions (upload schedule, style, complexity) before quoting — vague scopes lead to scope creep.
  • Don't ignore small creators — some of the best long-term retainers start under 5K followers.
